My Story.

Most recently I've been calling Anchorage, Alaska my home. However, home has always been a moving target. I grew up traveling and have been fortunate enough to have called five other countries my home at some point or another. Through these travels I've met a lot of people and heard a lot of stories. I can promise you, it never gets old. People and their stories are what drive me. Through design and engineering I share my stories and create ways for others to experience theirs.

I study Mechanical Engineering at Stanford University. I did so for my undergraduate and am now continuing as I pursue my masters degree. I am diving deep into mechatronics and expanding my breadth in product realization and materials. I have also found an affinity for teaching here. I work as a Teaching Assistant in the Stanford Product Realization Lab where I supervise and advise students in our maker lab and coach a small group of students in Stanford's Manufacturing and Design course. I have also been avidly involved in the Stanford outdoors community. I have taught classes in both class room and backcountry environments, lead recreational trips, and been the President of the Stanford Outdoors Council.

Beyond all this, I love climbing and backcountry skiing. I've also discovered a passion for comic books and strategy games.
